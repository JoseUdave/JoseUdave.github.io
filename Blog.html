About Skateboard

Skate, a sport known for using skateboards or skateboards. This instrument is a board that has in its lower part two axes that hold two wheels each. There are different types of skateboards, depending on the sports specialty you want to practice. The history of this discipline, originates in the sixties in the United States, when surfers from California were prohibited from practicing their sport at sea. They prepared a small surfboard with the wheels of some skates. This new sport spread quickly, reaching unimaginable success in such a short time, since by the seventies this sport was practiced all over the world.

The axis is formed by different parts -gums / Bushings -Base shaft -axis -Lifers / Risers For the different types of tables there are several measures of axes. For tables of 7.8 "to 8.1" are the heavier with a measurement of 5.25 "or 1.19. It is important to know that the weight and size of the axes will influence the ease to perform jumps or tricks.

vocabulary

  1. Skate: Skateboard
  2. Pop:Chop action against the ground with the nose or tail. It is also said about the ability of a "bounce" table, to do higher tricks.
  3. Trucks: Axes. Mechanisms that connect the wheels to the table by means of a suspension and a shaft with bearings.
  4. griptape: Rugged surface on the skate. It is used as a support for the feet on the skateboard, although it results in intense wear of the footwear.
  5. Half pipe: This expression refers to the inclined and usually high ramps, as if they were half of a circle, hence the name.
  6. Freestyle: Old section of skate competitions. It consisted in inventing tricks or combinations of tricks, generally very complex and that you showed in your turn. Currently it is a genre that includes various tricks.
  7. Old school tricks: Those that have something to do with the old free style and that, many times, do not have a name of their own. It is a category now very little practiced but in the seventies was in full swing.
  8. Nose: Front of the table. It can be differentiated because it is lower than the tail (back of the table).
  9. Tail: Back of the table.
  10. Nail: A term used to show that a grind or a lip was placed correctly.
  11. Ollie: A term used to jump
  12. Game of skate: Term that is used to refer to a battle of tricks with the skateboard that has its rules, between skaters, with their respective turns.
  13. Skateboarder: Said of the person riding a skateboard.
